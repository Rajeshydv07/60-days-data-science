# 🚀 Sprint 1 Review & Day 7 EDA 📊

We made it! Week 1 of the 60-Days Data Science Challenge is in the books. 

Today's focus was all about **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)**. Before jumping into complex modeling, you need to deeply understand the patterns hidden within your data. 

### 💡 What I Learned During Sprint 1:
- **Data Collection to Cleaning**: Sourcing data, identifying dirty records, filling missing values, and coercing correct types.
- **Visualizing Distributions**: Plotting histograms and boxplots uncovers what summary statistics (like means) try to hide. 
- **Identifying Outliers**: Real-world data is noisy. Identifying these anomalies early is critical so they don't skew future models.
- **The Value of EDA**: You can't just throw data at an algorithm. The insights generated from EDA shape feature engineering and algorithm selection.

### 🧗 What Challenges I Faced:
- Deciding between replacing missing values with medians vs. modes vs. dropping them entirely—context is everything!
- Wrestling with outliers: finding the line between a "dirty data point" and a "rare but valuable signal".
- Writing clean, reproducible Jupyter Notebooks that tell a cohesive data story.

Here are 5 key insights from today's EDA session:
1. **Age Distribution:** The age of customers is normally distributed, primarily centered around 35 years old.
2. **Income Outliers:** Income is clustered around $60,000, but boxplots clearly show a few high-income outliers (>$200,000) that need handling.
3. **Spending Score Range:** The spending score has a wide spread, mostly between 30 and 70.
4. **Category Popularity:** Customer preferences are evenly distributed across Electronics, Clothing, Groceries, and Home categories.
5. **No Linear Correlation:** There is no immediate visual linear correlation between Income and Spending Score, suggesting customer spending is driven by factors other than just their income level.
