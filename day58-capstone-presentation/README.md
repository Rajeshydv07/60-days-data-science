# Day 58: Capstone Demo & Presentation 🎤

Welcome to Day 58 of the 60 Days of Data Science challenge! Today focuses on the culmination of the capstone project: packaging the technical work into a compelling business presentation and demo.

## 🎯 Objectives
* Translate complex data engineering and ML architecture into business value.
* Create a professional presentation deck summarizing the problem, solution, and outcomes.
* Script and prepare a video demo walkthrough of the platform.
* Prepare project showcase materials for resumes and portfolios.

## 📁 Files in this Directory

* **`presentation_deck.html`**: A fully functional, interactive presentation deck built with HTML, CSS, and Reveal.js. Open this in any web browser to view the slides.
* **`demo_walkthrough_script.md`**: A step-by-step scene and dialogue script designed to guide the recording of the final project demo video.
* **`project_showcase_summary.md`**: Professional summaries and STAR-method breakdowns of the project, ready to be copy-pasted into a resume, LinkedIn profile, or used in interviews.

## 🚀 How to Use

1. **Review the Presentation:** Double-click `presentation_deck.html` to open it in your browser. Use the arrow keys to navigate the slides.
2. **Record the Demo:** Set up your local environment (FastAPI and Streamlit running), open `demo_walkthrough_script.md`, and use screen recording software (like OBS or Loom) to record your capstone presentation.
3. **Update Resume:** Open `project_showcase_summary.md` and integrate the bullet points into your professional portfolio.

## 🌟 Real-World Impact
Being a great Data Scientist or Machine Learning Engineer is only half the battle. The other half is communication. The ability to clearly present your system's architecture, justify your modeling decisions, and explain the direct business impact of your work is what separates junior developers from senior leaders.
